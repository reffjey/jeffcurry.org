happy birthday, mom

Happy birthday to mom, happy birthday to you
Congratulations on making it around the sun times 52

Or maybe I’ve got it twisted, and 1952 is what I meant
Anyway, thank you very much for my very first instrument

The saxophone taught me to love music, or maybe it was 92.7
Or most probably the croons and thrashes of Led Zeppelin

So many memories of listening to the radio as you ran through your chores
You’d hum along off-key to songs you only half remembered the words of

But somehow that made them sound better, like the melody had been rewritten with extra love
A drum lesson and a sizzler in the morning tricked me into following you spinning through so many store doors

At the time, it was frustrating, long, and arduous, what a long way to spend a Saturday
But at the same time, it taught me patience, and appreciation for the slower way

Sometimes I wish I could revert back to this version of me, with no concerns for the stressors of adult life
It felt nice living that life again a little bit when we went down to Orlando and you found out you liked garlic bread

So many things you discovered you liked this year, like mangos and garlic bread and tofu cooked many different ways
I personally discovered you liked Michael Jackson, and that Ted goes to the bathroom 90 times in the course of 4 days

I can’t wait to visit more places with you, and genuinely think you’ll find Japan to be absolutely great
You’ll be surprised by the efficiency, the trains, the snacks, the trees and birds and how everyone is a normal weight

We’ve still got so many places left on the list, so many meals we haven’t shared
So many trains to miss and museums to wander through, so many afternoons with nowhere to be and nothing to be scared

I’m glad you’ve been exercising more, expanding your peloton from 10 miles to 11, and doing situps every morning in bed
Sake and Sans cheer you on from the sidelines as you get your calisthenics done, but in actuality they just want to be fed

Sake purrs louder when you walk in the room, even louder than he does for me
Which is honestly a little rude, but I get it, that’s just the way it has to be

I hope that this year is the beginning of the years that you begin to find and support the life you want to live for yourself
Starting with getting that damn box of Vincent Van Gogh legos up onto the wall and no longer on top of the poop shelf

This year began with a boom, quite literally the chandelier falling down onto the ground it watched over for the past twenty years
With the air conditioning breaking for the third time in a year, there’s never been a better time for you to explore the world and face your fears

I’ll be there alongside you for the pieces that I can, and I’ll watch Sake and Dopey in all the moments that I cannot partake
I am so happy to see you enjoying life the best you possibly can and couldn’t be more proud of you not ever being a fake

The funny thing about getting older is realizing how much of who I am is just pieces of who you are
Your mannerisms, your laugh, your weird way of narrating like you’re in your own little memoir

I love you dearly for all that you’ve done for me and all the positivity you’ve shined on my life over the course of the growing number of years
I’ve been here I sincerely promise to always do absolutely everything I can to keep you as close as I can and near and dear
